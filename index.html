Mission Overview

UNLV ATLAS-1 combines real spaceflight with hands-on STEM training. Our team is building end-to-end capability: from early high-altitude balloon testing to an orbital CubeSat mission.

Objectives

UNLV ATLAS-1 is structured in stages: early testing with high-altitude balloon flights, followed by a full orbital CubeSat mission.

High-Altitude Balloon

  • Validate key subsystems (power, payload electronics, telemetry) in a near-space environment.
  • Exercise flight-like operations procedures and ground software.
  • Collect environmental and payload data to refine orbital mission requirements.

Orbital CubeSat

  • Deploy a 1U CubeSat into Low Earth Orbit to operate the student-developed payload.
  • Demonstrate end-to-end mission operations, from commanding to data downlink and analysis.
  • Train UNLV students across multiple disciplines in real spacecraft engineering.
